# Acceptable Use Policy

**ShortStay** — Last updated: [DATE]

This policy forms part of the [Terms of Service](./terms-of-service.md). It exists to protect the platform, other customers, and the people whose data lives in it — your guests.

---

## You must not

**Break the law.** No use of ShortStay to facilitate unlawful letting activity, fraud, money laundering, discrimination against guests (Equality Act 2010), or evasion of registration, licensing or planning obligations. Compliance tracking features are there to help you comply, not to help you appear to.

**Misuse guest data.** Guest records may only hold data you collected lawfully with proper notice to the guest. No scraping guest data from platforms in breach of their terms, no importing purchased lists, no using guest data for purposes your guests weren't told about, and no storing special category data (health, etc.) unless genuinely necessary and lawfully obtained (e.g. accessibility requirements a guest volunteered).

**Misuse the Xero integration.** No connecting Xero organisations you're not authorised to access, no attempting to pull data outside your approved scopes, and no extracting Xero API data for resale, benchmarking, or AI/ML training.

**Attack or probe the platform.** No penetration testing without written permission, no attempting to access other customers' data, no circumventing rate limits or seat limits, no automated scraping.

**Send spam.** Guest messaging features are for transactional and booking-related communication. Marketing messages to guests require your own compliance with PECR — consent or the soft opt-in, with an unsubscribe in every message.

**Resell or misrepresent.** No reselling access, no white-labelling without a written agreement, no claiming ShortStay output constitutes legal or tax advice to your own clients.

## What happens on breach

Minor or accidental issues: we'll contact you and work it out. Serious breaches — especially anything touching guest data misuse, security attacks, or unlawful activity — may result in immediate suspension while we investigate, termination under the Terms of Service, and where legally required, reports to the ICO or law enforcement.
