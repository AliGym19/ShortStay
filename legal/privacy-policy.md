## 1. Two roles, one platform

ShortStay wears two hats, and your rights depend on which applies to you:

- **We are the controller** for data about our customers (short-term rental operators) — your account, billing, support and usage data. This policy covers that.
- **We are the processor** for data about guests that our customers store in ShortStay. If you're a guest of a property managed through ShortStay, the operator is the controller — contact them first, and see our [Data Processing Addendum](./data-processing-addendum.md) for how we handle guest data on their behalf.

## 2. What we collect about you (operators)

| Data | Why | Lawful basis |
|---|---|---|
| Name, email, business name, address | Account creation, contract administration | Contract (Art. 6(1)(b)) |
| Billing details, payment history | Taking payment, accounting, tax | Contract; legal obligation |
| Xero connection metadata (organisation ID, OAuth tokens — encrypted) | Providing the Xero integration you've enabled | Contract |
| Usage data (features used, aggregate analytics) | Improving the product | Legitimate interests |
| Support correspondence | Resolving your issues | Contract; legitimate interests |
| Device/security data (IP, login events) | Fraud prevention, account security | Legitimate interests |

We do not collect special category data about operators and don't want it in account fields.

## 3. Xero data

When you connect Xero, we access only the scopes you approve. We use Xero API data solely to deliver the features of your approved use case, we protect it with at least industry-standard security measures as Xero's developer terms require, and **we never use it to train AI or machine learning models**. Disconnecting Xero stops all syncing; synced records already in ShortStay remain yours to keep or delete.

## 4. Who we share data with

- **Sub-processors** — hosting, payments and email infrastructure, listed at [DOMAIN]/subprocessors and flowed down under Article 28 terms.
- **Xero** — when you use the integration, data you push to Xero is governed by Xero's own privacy notice from the point it reaches them.
- **Authorities** — where the law requires.

We do not sell personal data or share it with advertisers.

## 5. International transfers

We host in [the UK / the EEA]. Where a sub-processor is outside the UK, we rely on UK adequacy regulations, or the UK IDTA / EU SCCs with the UK Addendum, supported by a transfer risk assessment.

## 6. Retention

- Account data: for the life of your subscription, then deleted within 30 days of the post-cancellation export window closing.
- Billing records: 6 years (HMRC requirement).
- Guest data: retained per your instructions as controller; deleted or returned at contract end under the DPA.
- Backups: rolled off within [35] days of deletion.

## 7. Your rights

You can ask us to access, correct, delete, restrict or port your data, and object to processing based on legitimate interests. Email [EMAIL] — we respond within one month (the clock pauses if we need to verify your identity, as the Data (Use and Access) Act 2025 confirms).

**Complaints:** under the DUAA (from 19 June 2026) you can complain to us directly and we must acknowledge within 30 days and respond without undue delay — use [EMAIL] with "data complaint" in the subject. You can also complain to the ICO at ico.org.uk at any time.

## 8. Security

Encryption in transit and at rest, OAuth tokens encrypted at the application layer, least-privilege access, audit logging, and documented security measures (which Xero may audit under its developer terms). If a breach creates risk to you, we'll notify the ICO within 72 hours and affected people without undue delay.

Covered separately in the [Cookie Policy](./cookie-policy.md). Short version: strictly necessary, security, first-party analytics and preference storage only — all within the PECR exemptions in force from 5 February 2026, with a free opt-out for analytics and preferences. No advertising trackers.
