This policy explains how ShortStay ("we", "us") uses cookies and similar storage and access technologies (local storage, session storage, pixels) on [DOMAIN] and within the ShortStay application.

---

## 1. What cookies are

Cookies are small text files placed on your device when you visit a website. We also use browser local storage to keep the app working between sessions. Both are covered by the same rules, and we treat them the same way in this policy.

## 2. The categories we use

### 2.1 Strictly necessary (no consent required)

These are essential for the service you've asked for. Under PECR they are exempt from consent.

| Name | Purpose | Duration |
|---|---|---|
| `shortstay_session` | Keeps you logged in | Session |
| `shortstay_csrf` | Security — prevents cross-site request forgery | Session |
| `xero_oauth_state` | Secures the OAuth 2.0 handshake when you connect your Xero account | 10 minutes |
| Local storage: `shortstay_state` | Holds app state (open views, unsaved drafts) so the app works | Until cleared |

### 2.2 Security and fraud prevention (no consent required)

Under the Data (Use and Access) Act 2025, technologies used solely to protect the security of your device, prevent or detect fraud, or automatically authenticate your identity are exempt from consent.

| Name | Purpose | Duration |
|---|---|---|
| `shortstay_device` | Recognises trusted devices to reduce repeated 2FA prompts | 30 days |

### 2.3 Analytics — statistical purposes (no consent required, opt-out available)

From 5 February 2026, first-party analytics used **solely** to collect statistical information to improve the service are exempt from consent, provided the data isn't shared with anyone else except to help improve the service.

Our analytics meet these conditions: they are first-party, aggregate, used only to improve ShortStay, and never used for advertising or shared with advertisers.

| Name | Purpose | Duration |
|---|---|---|
| `shortstay_analytics` | Aggregate usage statistics (pages visited, features used) | 13 months |

**Your right to opt out:** the law requires we give you a simple, free means of objecting. You can opt out at any time via **Settings → Privacy → Analytics**, or by emailing [EMAIL]. Opting out takes effect immediately.

### 2.4 Appearance and functionality preferences (no consent required, opt-out available)

Technologies used solely to remember your display and accessibility preferences are also exempt from 5 February 2026, subject to the same information and opt-out requirements.

| Name | Purpose | Duration |
|---|---|---|
| Local storage: `shortstay_prefs` | Theme, language, dashboard layout, accessibility settings | Until cleared |

You can clear these at any time via **Settings → Privacy** or your browser settings.

### 2.5 Advertising and third-party tracking

**We do not use advertising cookies, third-party trackers, or any technology that shares your data with advertisers.** If this ever changes, we will ask for your consent first — with "accept" and "reject" given equal prominence, and nothing set before you choose.

## 3. Consent, browser settings and objections

Because everything we currently set falls within a PECR exemption, we don't show a cookie consent banner. The law recognises browser settings and in-app controls as valid ways to signify your choices — you can:

- opt out of analytics and preference storage in **Settings → Privacy**
- block or delete cookies through your browser (note: blocking strictly necessary cookies will break login)

## 4. Personal data

Where a cookie involves personal data, the UK GDPR applies alongside PECR. Our [Privacy Policy](./privacy-policy.md) explains the lawful bases, retention and your rights.

## 5. Changes

We review our use of these technologies regularly, as the ICO's guidance expects. If we add a technology that requires consent, we'll ask before it's set and update this policy.
